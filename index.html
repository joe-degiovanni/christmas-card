üêï Meet Our New Golden Retriever Puppy -- Zygi

Kristina has been wanting a dog for a long time, and Joe has been resisting. In honor of the Vikings, we named him Zygi "Wolf" after Vikings' owner Zygi Wilf. He enjoys shoes, chewing on things, chasing chickens, barking at sheep, and carrying firewood all over the property.

He gave us a bit of a scare as a puppy as he had a battle with parvovirus, which it turns out can be fatal. He was in and out the vet for about a week being hooked up to an IV since he wouldn't eat or drink. He pulled through though, and he's been a healthy and happy puppy ever since.

Jim and Kristina brought him to obedience training this summer. Let's say he's a work in progress. In addition to basic obedience, he's learning to catch a frisbee. Also, He went on his first pheasant hunt this fall, and he did surprisingly well for not having any bird dog training.

üî¶ Spotlight on Joe

Joe has upgraded his homebrewing system and kegerator and has been brewing up a storm. Averaging one batch per month now, which is an increase from only a couple of batches per year since the kids were born. He enjoys the fruits of his labor while watching the Vikings, playing poker with friends, posthumously appreciating the humor of Norm MacDonald, or sitting on the patio watching for Northern Lights.

During deer hunting season, he got one of his best bucks ever, a decent sized 8-pointer. He has added a compound bow to his arsenal to extend his hunting season, and he's been practicing with it all summer.

He's still very slowly learning the ropes on the farm. We got a batch of 60 meat chickens this spring, and those are in the freezer now. We added a new set of egg layers too, and we're expecting those hens to start laying eggs any day now (fingers crossed). He tried shearing the sheep himself this year, and now fully appreciates being able to hire a professional to do it. The new chicken coop is working out great, and Joe and Jim have made some nice improvements to the fencing in the sheep barn and lambing jugs. He's still clueless in the garden.

Looking into 2024, Joe has plans to dabble in timber framing. He has acquired some books, simple project plans, and a few of the tools of the trade. A delivery of timbers from a sawyer in Little Falls are expected late December or early January. Check back next year to see how that goes!
